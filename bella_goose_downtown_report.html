1. Fix the 60% Drink-Only Problem — "Grab a Bite" Program

59.7% of orders are drink-only at $12.75 AOV. When customers add food, AOV jumps to $21.27 (+$8.52). Your natural food pairings are Scones + Latte (140 times) and Cinnamon Roll + Latte (120 times). This location has an even bigger attach problem than the main shop — and a shorter window to fix it.
Step-by-step:
1. Create a "Morning Combo" POS button: Any latte + GF Scone for $1.00 off — make it one tap for the barista
2. Place scones and cinnamon rolls in a visible display case RIGHT at the register (not behind the counter)
3. Barista script: "Would you like a fresh scone with that? They pair perfectly with the Salted Caramel" — every single order
4. Add a "Combo" section to any menu board: Latte + Scone, Latte + Cinnamon Roll, Smoothie + Banana Bread
5. Target: move food attach from 16.2% to 25% in peak months
6. Math: if 10% of drink-only orders add food = 888 orders x $8.52 = $7,566 incremental

2. Maximize Saturday + Sunday (52% of Revenue)

Saturday ($53.9K) and Sunday ($44.9K) together are 52% of all revenue. Saturday AOV ($13.20) and Sunday AOV ($13.83) are the highest of the week. During peak months, these weekend days are likely packed with tourists. Are you fully staffed and stocked?
Step-by-step:
1. Ensure full staff on every Sat/Sun during Jul–Aug — never short a barista on your money days
2. Pre-prep extra scones, cinnamon rolls, and banana bread on Friday for weekend rush
3. If lines are long (likely at 8–9am peak): add a "grab and go" station — pre-made cold brews, bagged scones, retail items
4. Consider a weekend-only special or "Downtown Signature" drink at premium pricing ($8+)
5. Ensure retail display (beans, syrups, hoodies) is fully stocked and visible on weekends when tourist foot traffic peaks

SECTION B Cost Reduction + Efficiency
With only $188K in revenue, every dollar of waste matters more. The shoulder months (May, Sep) are where margins get thin.
CRITICAL

5. Fix May Ramp-Up Losses — 38.7% Labor Cost

May: $16.6K revenue but $6.4K in labor (38.7%). Revenue is only $535/day with 40 orders/day — but you are running 464 labor hours. That is $35.77 revenue per labor hour, less than half of August. The problem: you are fully staffing and training before customers arrive.
Step-by-step:
1. Delay full staffing until late May (Memorial Day weekend). Run skeleton crew the first 3 weeks: 1 shift leader + 1 barista
2. Do all training BEFORE opening day on the company dime at the main location — do not train during the revenue season
3. Open limited hours in early May (7am–1pm only, closed Tue/Wed) and expand as volume builds
4. Set a May labor budget of $4K (vs current $6.4K) — save $2.4K
5. September: mirror the approach in reverse. Cut to limited hours after Labor Day. Last 2 weeks: skeleton crew only.
6. Combined shoulder savings: $3–5K

1. In May and September: close on Tuesday (or reduce to 7am–12pm only)
2. In June: run reduced hours Tue/Wed (7am–2pm instead of full day)
3. In Jul–Aug: keep Tue/Wed open but run minimal afternoon staff (2 people max after noon)
4. Redirect those labor hours to bolstering Saturday/Sunday coverage
