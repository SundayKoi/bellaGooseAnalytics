Maximize the Retail Window

1. Cut Discounts from 13.9% to Under 5%

$14,143 in discounts on $88K revenue is alarming — that is nearly $1 out of every $7. Audit every active discount: employee meals, loyalty comps, damaged items, "friends and family" markdowns. Set a weekly discount budget of $150 (down from the current $272/week average during retail season). Require manager approval for any single discount over $10. Track discount reasons in Square to identify patterns.
Estimated impact: $7–10K/yr recovered revenue

2. Extend Operating Days to Include Thursday and Friday

The shop barely operates mid-week (only 1–3 days each for Tue–Thu). Friday already shows decent traffic at $13.5K total. Adding consistent Thursday and Friday hours during peak tourist months (June–August) could capture weekday tourist traffic. Friday averages $900/day when open — adding 12 more Fridays at even $700/day = $8.4K. Test Thursdays at reduced hours (7–11 AM only) to manage labor risk.
Estimated impact: $5–12K/yr additional revenue

3. Open the Retail Window Earlier (Late April)

Production labor is already running in March–April at $14–15K/month with zero retail revenue. Opening the retail window in late April (even weekends only) captures shoulder-season tourists and early-season locals. Staff is already on-site for production — adding a barista shift is incremental. Even 8 weekend days in late April at $800/day adds $6.4K.
Estimated impact: $4–8K/yr additional revenue

Optimize Products and Pricing

4. Push Food Combos to Raise AOV from $17.46 to $20+

Briggsville already has a strong food game (52% food revenue vs 48% drinks). Lean into this with breakfast combos: "Sammie + Brew" for $12 (vs $12+ a la carte) that feel like a deal but guarantee a 2-item ticket. Train baristas to suggest a scone or cinnamon roll with every drink order. The Cheddar Bacon Sammie at $9 already outsells every drink — make it the anchor of a "Briggsville Breakfast" combo.
Estimated impact: $3–5K/yr from higher AOV

5. Price Specialty Lattes $0.25–0.50 Higher

The Salted Caramel Latte (1,405 units) is your volume leader at $5.50–6.25. In a tourist destination, a $0.25 increase to $5.75–6.50 on all specialty lattes (Salted Caramel, Vanilla Bean, Butter Pecan, Honey Cinnamon, Lavender Mint) is unlikely to dent volume. That covers about 3,000 specialty drinks at $0.25 = $750 in pure margin.
Estimated impact: $750–1.5K/yr

6. Monetize Alt-Milk Modifiers

Oat (507 orders) and Almond (243 orders) milk are popular. If not already charging a $0.75 upcharge for alt-milks, implement one. At 750 alt-milk orders/year at $0.75 = $562 in pure margin. Post the upcharge clearly on the menu. For regulars, consider a "loyalty alt-milk" card where every 10th alt-milk drink is free.
Estimated impact: $500–750/yr

Strategic Improvements

7. Sell Retail Bags of Beans More Aggressively

You have an on-site roaster ($25K/yr labor). Bean bags sold 132 units at $17.86 avg = $2,324. This is underperforming for a location that literally roasts beans. Display beans prominently at the register. Offer a "buy a bag, get $1 off your next drink" card. Tourist customers who discover a great roast will buy bags to take home — this is high-margin revenue with zero additional production labor.
Estimated impact: $1–2K/yr from increased bean sales

8. Track Production Output Value Separately

The $220K in production labor looks alarming against $88K retail revenue, but it supports all Bella Goose locations. Implement internal transfer pricing in Square or a simple spreadsheet: when production ships baked goods, syrups, or beans to other locations, log the wholesale value. This reveals the true margin of the production hub and helps you make informed decisions about scaling production vs outsourcing.
Estimated impact: Better decision-making across all locations
