1. "Bella Goose Breakfast" Combo — Attack the 46% Drink-Only Problem

During tourist season, 46.4% of orders are drink-only ($15.37 AOV). When tourists add food, AOV jumps to $47.65 — a $32.28 increase. Tourists are already spending; they just need a nudge. The natural #1 pairing is Salted Caramel Latte + Cheddar Bacon Sammie (2,373 times/year).
Step-by-step implementation:
1. Create 3 named combos on the POS: "The Bella Goose" (Salted Caramel + Bacon Sammie, $1.50 off), "The Mountain Morning" (Vanilla Bean + Avocado Toast, $1.50 off), "The Tourist Special" (Latte + Build Your Own Waffle, $1.50 off)
2. Print a tent card for every table and the register: "First time in town? Try the Bella Goose Breakfast — our most popular combo"
3. Train baristas: when a tourist orders just a drink, say "Have you tried our Bella Goose Breakfast? It's our #1 combo — comes with our famous Cheddar Bacon Sammie"
4. Add combos to any online ordering/menu boards with photos
5. Track daily: food attach rate by shift. Post results in back of house. Target: move from 28.8% to 40% during peak season
6. If 20% of peak drink-only orders convert: 3,838 orders × $32.28 = $123,866 incremental revenue

SECTION B Off-Season Survival & Local Loyalty
Off-season is about the locals. They're your year-round base — 42,576 orders in off-season, mostly weekends. The strategy here is different: lean operations, local-focused menu, and community engagement. You're not trying to match summer; you're trying to stay profitable.
OFF-SEASON — CRITICAL

5. Cut Midweek Off-Season Hours — Wednesday & Thursday are Bleeding

Off-season Wednesday: $3,444/day revenue with $1,351 labor cost (39.2% labor!). Thursday: $3,169/day with 35.8% labor. Revenue per labor hour is $38-41 on these days — nearly half of peak season. Meanwhile, off-season weekends are great: 14-15% labor with $82-90/hr efficiency.
Step-by-step implementation:
1. Starting November 1: reduce Wed/Thu shifts by 2 positions (1 barista + 1 kitchen)
2. Consider closing the kitchen on Wednesday after 11am (keeping drinks-only with 2 baristas)
3. Or: close entirely on the slowest off-season day (likely Thursday) — many tourist-town businesses do this and locals understand. Revenue at risk: ~$3,169/day, but labor saved: ~$1,135/day + COGS + utilities
4. If staying open: schedule minimum crew (2 baristas, 1 shift leader, no dedicated kitchen on slow days — offer a limited cold food menu)
5. Redirect those labor hours to bolstering Saturday/Sunday coverage where revenue is $8,200+/day at 14% labor cost
6. Target: bring off-season midweek labor below 25% (from 35-39%)

COST — YEAR-ROUND

8. Scale Labor with the Season — Create a Staffing Calendar

Peak: 129 labor hrs/day, $1,627 labor/day, 17.1% of revenue. Off-season: 37 hrs/day, $502/day, 23.4% of revenue. The problem: labor scaled 3.5x from off-season to peak, but revenue scaled 4.5x. Labor isn't elastic enough.
Step-by-step implementation:
1. Create 3 staffing templates: "Peak" (Jun-Aug), "Shoulder" (Apr-May, Sep-Oct), "Off-Season" (Nov-Mar)
2. Peak template: full staff, max hours, hire seasonal workers (college students, summer workers)
3. Shoulder template: 75% of peak hours — reduce by 1-2 positions per day
4. Off-season template: 50% of peak hours — skeleton crew weekdays, near-full on weekends
5. Set labor budget targets: Peak 17%, Shoulder 20%, Off-season 20% (currently 23.4%)
6. Review weekly: if revenue/labor hour drops below $55, cut a shift the next week
7. If off-season labor drops from 23.4% to 20% target: saves $26.5K on $782K off-season revenue

4. Create staggered shifts so there's always an overlap for smooth handoff
