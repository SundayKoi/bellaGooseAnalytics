📈 Revenue Growth Actions
Estimated total impact: $200–350K in additional annual revenue
REVENUE — HIGH IMPACT

1. Food Attach Program — "Add a Bite"

46.7% of orders are drink-only at $14.88 AOV. When a customer adds food, AOV jumps to $43.37 — a $28.48 increase. This is your single biggest revenue lever.

Specific steps:
• Create POS prompts: "Add a Cheddar Bacon Sammie for $2 off?" when drink-only order is placed
• Train baristas on the top combos: Salted Caramel + Cheddar Bacon (2,373 natural pairings), Vanilla Bean + Cheddar Sausage (1,266 pairings)
• Place food displays near the register — visual cues at the point of decision
• Set a measurable target: move food attach rate from 25.2% to 35% within 90 days
• Track daily by shift leader — post results in back of house
↑ $120–200K/year if attach rate reaches 35%
REVENUE — HIGH IMPACT

2. Weekend Brunch Expansion

Sat/Sun = 42% of revenue with 18% higher AOV ($22 vs $18.50). These are your most profitable days and your peak hours are 7-10am MT. There's room to extend the brunch rush.

Specific steps:
• Launch weekend-only premium items: special waffles, seasonal bennys, brunch cocktails (if licensed)
• Promote a "Weekend Family Deal" bundle — 2 drinks + 2 food items at a slight discount to drive multi-item orders
• Extend kitchen hours on weekends to capture the 10am-12pm crowd ($530K in "late morning" revenue)
• Use social media to promote weekend specials every Thursday/Friday
• Add 1 extra barista + 1 extra line cook on Sat/Sun mornings
↑ $30–50K/year
REVENUE — MEDIUM IMPACT

3. Winter Seasonal Menu & Retail Push

Revenue drops 52% from summer to winter, but retail (Beans + Syrup) stays strong at $10-13K/month year-round. Winter-specific items like "Flannel" ($10K in just 3 months) show seasonal menu appetite.

Specific steps:
• Develop 3-4 winter-exclusive drinks (peppermint mocha, gingerbread latte, winter chai) launching Oct 1
• Create holiday gift bundles: Beans + Syrup + Mug for $25-35 — marketed as "Bella Goose at Home"
• Run a holiday gift card promotion: "Buy $50, get $10 free" to drive January traffic
• Partner with local businesses for corporate gift orders (bean bags, syrup sets)
• The Flannel drink did ~$10K in 3 winter months — expand this concept with 2-3 more seasonal offerings
↑ $30–50K/year in winter revenue
REVENUE — QUICK WIN

4. Alt-Milk Premium Pricing

Oat milk is ordered 15,204 times/year (2.5x almond at 6,160). These are premium-seeking customers who expect to pay more for alternatives.

Specific steps:
• If not already charging: add $0.75 upcharge for oat, almond, and skim milk
• If already charging: verify the upcharge is at least $0.75 (many shops now charge $1.00+)
• Add "+1 Shot" upsell prompt — 4,161 customers already add shots voluntarily
• Consider "Upgrade to Large" prompt at POS for another $0.75-1.00
• Display "customize your drink" menu board highlighting premium options
↑ $16–25K/year
REVENUE — MEDIUM IMPACT

5. Afternoon Revenue Recovery

After 12pm MT, revenue drops sharply — the afternoon (2-5pm) generates only $152K all year. This is underutilized capacity with staff already on-site.

Specific steps:
• Launch an afternoon happy hour (2-4pm): discounted drinks or a "cookie + coffee" combo
• Add grab-and-go cold items: bottled cold brew, pre-made smoothies, packaged snacks
• Target the afternoon work crowd with a "study session" special and loyalty stamp
• Promote afternoon retail (beans, syrups) with a "bring Bella Goose home" afternoon display
• Reduce food prep labor after 1pm but keep full barista coverage
↑ $20–30K/year
REVENUE — QUICK WIN

6. Gift Card & Loyalty Program Push

Gift cards generated only $12.6K in revenue but have zero processing fees (vs 3.01% on cards). Plus 1,252 transactions already use Square Gift Cards. Every dollar shifted to gift cards saves 3%.

Specific steps:
• Offer "Buy $25 gift card, get $5 bonus" promotion — drives prepaid revenue
• Sell gift cards prominently at the register with attractive physical cards
• Promote gift cards for birthdays, holidays, teacher appreciation, corporate gifting
• If not using Square Loyalty: launch a simple "Buy 10, get 1 free" stamp program
• Set goal: double gift card volume to $25K → saves $750+ in processing fees alone
↑ $10–15K/year + fee savings
💵 Cost Reduction Actions
Estimated total impact: $70–120K in annual cost savings
COST — CRITICAL

7. Winter Staffing Optimization

Labor cost as % of revenue jumps from 16.4% (July) to 26.4% (December). Revenue dropped $167K from peak to December, but labor only dropped $13K. That's $35-50K in excess winter labor.

Specific steps:
• Create two seasonal scheduling tiers: "Summer" (Jun-Aug) and "Winter" (Nov-Feb)
• Winter tier: reduce weekly scheduled hours by 20% — go from ~90 hrs/day to ~72 hrs/day
• Use part-time baristas for flexibility — reduce from 5 to 3-4 per morning shift in winter
• Cut afternoon shifts first (post-1pm revenue is minimal in winter)
• Target: bring winter labor below 20% of revenue (from current 25-26%)
• Potential savings: if Nov-Feb labor matched 20% target = $25-35K saved
↓ $25–40K/year saved
COST — HIGH IMPACT

8. Midweek Schedule Tightening

Wednesday has the worst revenue-per-labor-hour ($49/hr) vs Sunday at $94/hr. Tuesday-Thursday labor costs 24-27% of revenue vs weekend 13-15%. Midweek is overstaffed.

Specific steps:
• Audit Tue-Thu schedules: reduce by 1 barista and 1 kitchen staff per day
• Close kitchen 1 hour earlier on Tue-Wed (lowest food demand days)
• Cross-train shift leaders to cover kitchen during slow midweek periods
• Set midweek labor target: $60/hr revenue per labor hour (from current $49-54)
• Monitor the dashboard weekly — if revenue/hr drops below $55, cut a shift that day
↓ $15–25K/year saved
COST — MEDIUM IMPACT

9. Overtime & Long Shift Controls

24.2% of shifts exceed 8 hours — that's 1,427 shifts with 903 excess hours costing $16.4K. Managers average the longest shifts (250+ excess hours).

Specific steps:
• Set 8-hour shift caps in Square Labor for baristas and shift leaders
• Require manager approval for any shift extension beyond 8 hours
• Review Manager role: 145 shifts with 250+ excess hours — are managers staying because they have to, or because there's no handoff plan?
• Create staggered shift schedules so there's always overlap for handoff
• Track overtime weekly and share with team — awareness alone reduces it
↓ $10–16K/year saved
COST — OPERATIONAL

10. Processing Fee Reduction Strategy

$66.4K/year in fees at 3.01%. Small orders under $10 pay an effective 5.0% rate ($7.4K in fees on $148K volume). Cash has zero fees.

Specific steps:
• Incentivize cash for small orders: "Pay cash on orders under $10, save 5%" sign at register
• Promote gift card preloading — zero processing fees per transaction
• Negotiate with Square: at $2M+ annual volume, request a volume discount or custom rate
• Evaluate competitor processors (Toast, Clover) for rate comparison
• Consider a small-order minimum for card payments ($3-5 minimum)
• Even a 0.25% rate reduction = $4,900/year in savings
↓ $5–10K/year saved
COST — OPERATIONAL

11. Discount Audit & Controls

$82.4K in annual discounts across 7,242 orders. Average discount is $11.37 per order. Gift Cards, A La Carte, and Salted Caramel Latte are the most-discounted items. Who is applying these and why?

Specific steps:
• Pull a report by employee of who applies the most discounts — look for outliers
• Set discount approval tiers: baristas can apply up to $5, shift leaders up to $15, managers unlimited
• Require a reason code for every discount (comp, loyalty reward, complaint, employee meal)
• Review the "A La Carte Options" discounting ($4.4K) — is this a system configuration issue?
• Target: reduce total discounts by 25% = $20K saved without impacting customer experience
↓ $15–20K/year saved
COST — QUICK WIN

12. Meeting & Training Cost Review

182 meeting shifts ($6.4K) and 89 training shifts ($5.8K) at full pay. 39 employees had meeting shifts — that's a lot of paid meeting time.

Specific steps:
• Audit meeting frequency and duration — are 182 paid meeting shifts necessary?
• Move recurring meetings to shift overlap times so they don't require extra clock-in
• Consolidate training sessions — 30 employees trained across 89 shifts could be more efficient
• Use pre-shift huddles (5-10 min) instead of separate meeting shifts where possible
• Target: reduce meeting hours by 50% = $3.2K saved
↓ $3–6K/year saved
