Revenue Recovery — Stop Leaving Money on the Table

1. Investigate and Fix the 14% Cancellation Rate

In 2023 your cancellation rate was essentially zero. By 2025 it hit 14% — that is roughly 16,000 cancelled orders. If even half of those represented real demand that walked away, that could be $170K+ in lost revenue. Dig into Square to find: Are these mobile/online orders that never get picked up? Kitchen overload causing timeouts? A workflow where staff cancel instead of modify? Pull a sample of 50 cancelled orders and categorize the reasons. This is your single biggest revenue leak.
Estimated impact: $85–170K/yr if cut to 5%

2. Reverse the Declining Food Attach Rate (63% down to 57%)

In 2023, 63.4% of drink orders also included food. By 2025 it dropped to 56.9%. On ~150K drink orders per year, each percentage point is roughly 1,500 missed food items at $8–14 each. Thats $12–21K per point. The 6.5-point decline represents $78–137K in annual food revenue that stopped happening. Possible causes: menu changes (the Egg Sammie cratered), speed-of-service issues during rush, or counter layout changes. Test a "combo prompt" where the POS suggests a food item with every drink order. Train baristas to mention one food item by name.
Estimated impact: $40–80K/yr if restored to 60%+

3. Audit the $538K Annual Discount Spend

You gave away $538K in discounts in 2025 on $2.12M in revenue. Thats $1 discounted for every $4 collected. The good news: your rate improved from 6.7% to 3–4%. The bad news: at $2M+ revenue, even 3–4% is $60–80K per quarter. Categorize every discount type in Square: employee meals, loyalty, damaged items, manager comps, promotions. Set quarterly budgets by category. The biggest opportunity is likely in reducing blanket percentage-off promotions and moving to targeted offers (e.g., free cookie with $20+ order vs 15% off everything).
Estimated impact: $30–60K/yr by cutting rate another 1–2 points

4. Diagnose the Egg Sammie Cliff ($139K to $14K)

The Egg Sammie went from your #1 food item at $139K (2024) to $14K (2025) — a 90% drop. The Cheddar Bacon and Cheddar Sausage Sammie variants together did $115K + $92K, so demand for breakfast sandwiches clearly exists. If this was a deliberate menu consolidation, verify that the replacement items fully captured the revenue. If not deliberate, this is $125K in annual revenue that evaporated. Similarly, "Smoothie" dropped from $47K to $3.6K and "BST and Chips" from $37K to $7K. Ensure these removals were intentional and that alternatives are capturing the demand.
Estimated impact: $25–50K/yr if gaps are identified and filled

Revenue Growth — Lean Into What Is Working

5. Double Down on Retail Products (Beans + Syrups)

Retail products generated $319K over 3 years (Beans $175K, Syrups $144K) and are growing fast: Retail Syrup jumped +40% in 2025, Holiday Syrup Boxes +140%. These are high-margin items with no additional kitchen labor. Create a dedicated retail display near the register. Bundle a "Tourist Take-Home" pack (1 bag beans + 1 syrup bottle) at a slight discount. Push syrup gift boxes year-round, not just holidays. At current growth rates, retail could hit $100K/yr by 2026 without intervention — with focused effort, $130–150K is realistic.
Estimated impact: $20–40K/yr incremental

6. Expand the Waffle Program

Waffles are a powerhouse: Classic ($177K), Chocolate Covered Strawberry ($160K), Build Your Own ($150K) — nearly $500K combined over 3 years. Build Your Own grew +39% and Plain Waffle +63% in 2025. Consider a "Waffle of the Month" special to drive repeat visits from locals. Add a savory waffle option to capture lunch traffic. The waffle station is already staffed — incremental menu items have near-zero additional labor cost.
Estimated impact: $15–30K/yr from expanded waffle menu

7. Capture More Weekend Revenue (92% Premium)

Weekends average $8,565/day vs $4,487/day on weekdays — a 92% premium. Saturday and Sunday alone generated $891K in 2025 (42% of revenue). Optimize for weekends: ensure full staffing (no gaps from call-outs), pre-prep popular items Friday night, consider a weekend-only special menu item. If you can increase weekend throughput by just 5% through better prep and staffing, thats $44K/yr.
Estimated impact: $20–45K/yr

8. Push Shoulder Season Growth

Your best YoY growth was in shoulder months: May (+34%), April (+29%), October (+49% in 2024). Shoulder season already contributes 25% of revenue. These months have available capacity (not maxed like July/August) and lower labor costs. Run targeted local marketing in April/May and Sep/Oct: loyalty rewards for locals, "first waffle of spring" campaign, fall harvest specials. The shoulder is where you can grow without hitting peak-season capacity constraints.
Estimated impact: $15–30K/yr

Strategic Improvements

9. Address Employee Turnover (47% annual loss)

In 2024, 30 of 64 employees left (47%). At an estimated $1,500–2,500 in hiring/training cost per replacement, that is $45–75K in hidden annual cost. More importantly, experienced staff are faster and upsell better. Conduct stay interviews with your top 10 performers. Common retention levers in food service: predictable scheduling, small raises for 6+ month tenure, shift meal improvements, tip pooling transparency. Even reducing turnover by 10 percentage points saves $15–25K in direct costs plus improves service quality.
Estimated impact: $15–25K/yr in reduced turnover costs

10. Negotiate Processing Fees (Rising from 2.81% to 3.05%)

Processing fees hit $65K in 2025 and the rate is creeping up. At $2M+ in card volume, you have leverage to negotiate with Square or explore alternatives. Even a 0.15% reduction on $1.7M in card payments saves $2,550/yr. More impactful: if your 17% cash rate drops further (industry trend), fees will grow faster. Consider a small cash discount (e.g., "save 3% with cash") to maintain cash volume, or negotiate volume pricing with Square.
Estimated impact: $2.5–5K/yr
