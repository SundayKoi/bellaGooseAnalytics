High Priority — Immediate Revenue Recovery

1. Reverse the Declining Food Attach Rate Critical

In 2023, 63.4% of drink orders also included food. By 2025 it dropped to 56.9%. On ~150K drink orders per year, each percentage point is roughly 1,500 missed food items at $8–14 each ($12–21K per point). The 6.5-point decline represents $78–137K in annual food revenue that stopped happening. Investigate: Did the Egg Sammie discontinuation cause this? Are baristas skipping food suggestions during rush? Has the menu board layout changed? Implement a "combo prompt" in the POS and train baristas to name-suggest one food item with every drink order.

2. Tighten Discount Controls — Maintain the 3.7% Rate as You Scale High

Discounts totaled $81K in 2025 on $2.2M gross — a 3.7% rate, improved from 4.1% in prior years. This is healthy, but at your growth rate the dollar amount will climb fast if the rate creeps back up. Categorize every discount type in Square: employee meals, loyalty, damaged items, manager comps, promotions. Set quarterly budgets by category. The goal is not aggressive cutting but preventing regression — holding at 3.5% as you grow to $2.5M+ would save $12–15K vs letting it drift back to 4.1%. Move from blanket percentage-off promotions to targeted offers (e.g., free cookie with $20+ order vs 10% off everything).

8. Address 47% Annual Employee Turnover Strategic

Common retention levers: predictable scheduling, tenure-based raises ($0.50/hr at 6 months), shift meal improvements.
